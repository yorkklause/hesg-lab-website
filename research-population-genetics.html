Population genetics

Approach

Reconstructing human genetic history

Human genetic variation carries signatures of migration, admixture, introgression, drift, and selection. We develop population-genetic methods to reconstruct these histories and quantify how they shape ancestry structure across present-day groups.

This research includes human population history reconstruction, new models for complex admixture, and scalable methods for studying modern and archaic ancestry in biobank-scale genomic data.

Questions

What this area asks

Population history reconstruction

How can we infer multi-wave admixture, migration, and ancestry formation from genome-wide variation?

Method development

How can population-genetic models be made more flexible, scalable, and useful for modern genomic cohorts?

Evolutionary processes

How do selection, introgression, and demographic history shape genetic variation across human populations?
