Student Opportunities

Graduate and Undergraduate Students

Graduate Students

The HESG Lab welcomes graduate students interested in statistical genetics, population genetics, human genetics, computational biology, biostatistics, computer science, mathematics, data science, and related quantitative disciplines.

Students in the laboratory receive interdisciplinary training in:

  • Statistical genetics and human genetics
  • Population and evolutionary genetics
  • Bayesian statistics and machine learning
  • Scientific programming and high-performance computing
  • Reproducible research and open science
  • Scientific writing and grant development

Graduate students are encouraged to develop independent research questions while working collaboratively on projects involving psychiatric genetics, population genomics, cross-ancestry analyses, and statistical method development.

Students are typically admitted through the appropriate Indiana University graduate programs. Prospective students interested in joining the laboratory are encouraged to contact Dr. Kai Yuan before applying.

Undergraduate Students

Motivated undergraduate students are welcome to participate in ongoing research projects. Students will receive hands-on research experience in computational genomics, statistical genetics, and scientific programming while working closely with graduate students and postdoctoral fellows.
