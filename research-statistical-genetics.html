Statistical genetics

Approach

Post-GWAS discovery and interpretation

Genome-wide association studies identify loci, but translating association signals into variants, genes, and mechanisms requires statistical models designed for post-GWAS inference. We develop methods for fine-mapping, association testing, variant prioritization, and genetic architecture analysis.

This area focuses on discovering genetic factors for complex traits and diseases, including inflammatory, psychiatric, and other human disorders where causal signals are distributed across many loci and biological pathways.

Questions

What this area asks

Fine-mapping methods

How can we improve credible sets, causal variant discovery, and cross-study interpretation after GWAS?

Genetic association studies

How can association models better detect genetic effects for complex traits and diseases across cohorts?

Disease-gene discovery

How can post-GWAS signals be connected to genes, pathways, tissues, and disease biology?
